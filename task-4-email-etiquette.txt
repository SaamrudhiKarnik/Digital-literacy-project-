Responsible Social Media Use: A College Student's Checklist
DO'S
	DON'TS
	Do customize your privacy settings to limit who can see your posts, personal information, and tagged photos.
	Don't post your real-time location (e.g., "I'm on vacation for a week")—it can compromise your safety and privacy.
	Do think before you post—consider whether a potential employer, professor, or family member would be comfortable seeing your content.
	Don't engage with online trolls or strangers in heated arguments; it rarely ends well and can harm your reputation.
	Do use a strong, unique password for each platform and enable two-factor authentication to protect your accounts.
	Don't share sensitive personal information like your student ID number, home address, or financial details publicly.
	Do fact-check before sharing news or articles to avoid spreading misinformation or fake news.
	Don't post negative comments about professors, classmates, or your institution—it can have real-world consequences.
	Do take regular breaks from social media to prioritize mental health, academics, and in-person connections.
	Don't compare your behind-the-scenes life to someone else's highlight reel—it can negatively impact your self-esteem.
